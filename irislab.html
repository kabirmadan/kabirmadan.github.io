Shape Sensing in Soft Robots Using Optical Fibers

  • Category:

    Research

  • Lab:

    Innovative Robotics and Interactive Systems Lab

  • Team Size:

    3 (myself, a graduate student mentor, and Dr. Margaret Coad)

  • Skills:

    CAD (SolidWorks); Programming (MATLAB, Python); Technical writing; Experimental design and procedure

  • Main accomplishments:

    I was awarded a four-figure research grant by the Center for Undergraduate Scholarly Engagement through a competitive application process.

  • Description:

    I am working on implementing a novel shape sensing technique for soft vine robots which uses optical fibers to measure strain along the length of the robot. Using Euler-Bernoulli beam theory, this strain data is used to sense local curvature of the body. My specific work has to do with improving the fabrication process for this system, as well as quantifying the accuracy of its readings.

    • I am working on finishing up writing an academic journal article regarding this work. Stay tuned!
