Fidget Spinner Mini-Project

  • Summary

    I individually designed and generated the CNC tool paths for a fidget spinner toy. I then assembled a physical prototype.

  • Skills

    CAD (SolidWorks); CAM (SolidWorks); Engineering drawings; Physical prototyping;

  • Team Size

    1 (myself)

  • Category

    Coursework: Design Tools II (AME 21268)

  • Detailed Breakdown

    The goal of this project was to get hands-on experience with computer aided manufacturing (CAM). I was provided by my professor with 0.25” HDPE stock, a single 0.35” deep bearing, 1/4-20 hex nuts for weights, and plastic finger pads; given these materials, I was tasked with designing and manufacturing a fidget spinner.

    It is important to consider what the desired behavior of a fidget spinner looks like — high rotational speed, long spin duration, or a unique artistic design are some of the possible directions which can inform the design of such a device. I decided that it was important that my device be optimized for rotational speed. As such, my design was motivated by the idea of concentrating a large amount of mass very close to the center of rotation so as to create a relatively small mass moment of inertia. To achieve this, I decided to place six ‘weights’ (steel nuts) in a ring immediately surrounding the finger pads. For geometric consistency, the outer shell of the device was also designed to be hexagonal.

    I created a model based on this idea, with holes toleranced to allow the steel weighting nuts to be press-fit into their holes. I added additional circular holes near the outer edge of the fidget spinner to shift the weight distribution to be concentrated even nearer to the center hole.
